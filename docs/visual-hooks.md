# Visual Hooks — Image Design Rules per Platform

> What catches the eye in the first 0.5 seconds on each platform. Engineer into every media brief. Updated 2026-07-07.

---

## Why this doc exists

Captions have hooks. Images need them too. A scroll-stopping post has **two hooks**: one in the text (first line) and one in the image (visual pattern interrupt). Without both, you're relying on a single channel — algorithm or human thumb moves you past.

This doc codifies what makes an image stop the scroll on each platform. Every media brief must reference these rules.

---

## Universal rules (all platforms)

1. **Mobile-first.** 60%+ of all social traffic is mobile. Optimize for thumbnail readability.
2. **High contrast.** Bold colors + clear subject = instant recognition in <1s.
3. **Minimal text on image.** Under 30 words. Centered. In the "safe zone" (middle 50% of frame).
4. **Strong focal point.** One subject, center or rule-of-thirds. No clutter.
5. **Brand consistency.** Same palette + fonts across posts. Trains the eye to recognize you.
6. **No real faces, no logos.** Trademark + deepfake/misleading conduct risk.

## LinkedIn

**Tone:** Professional, trustworthy, business-relevant. Scan-fast.

| Category | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Color palette** | Deep blues, greys, whites. Avoid neon / party-like colors. High contrast between bg + text. |
| **Composition** | 1080×1080 (1:1 square) or 1200×1350 (4:5 portrait). Portrait takes more feed space — use it. |
| **Text overlay** | 2-3 words max. Center-aligned. Within center "safe zone." Sans-serif. |
| **Focal point** | Center 50% of frame. Bold subject (face, product, graphic). Strong contrast. |

**What catches the eye:**
- Single bold face or product shot centered
- Clean geometry (architectural, geometric patterns)
- High contrast between subject and background
- Professional color palette (navy, slate, white, warm grey)

**Banned on LinkedIn:**
- Stock-feel images
- Overly saturated colors
- Multiple focal points competing
- Tiny text that disappears at thumbnail size

## Instagram

**Tone:** Visually bold, emotional, trend-driven. Aesthetic matters.

| Category | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Color palette** | Vibrant, saturated, high contrast. Match trends (neon, pastel, monochrome). |
| **Composition** | 1080×1080 (1:1) preferred. Portrait 4:5 also works for mobile reach. |
| **Text overlay** | 1-line headlines in stylish fonts (sans-serif, brush). Under 30 words. |
| **Focal point** | Center or rule-of-thirds. High-contrast lighting. Dynamic posing ok. |

**What catches the eye:**
- Bold colors that pop in feed (yellow on dark, red on white, etc.)
- One clear subject, isolated from background
- Atmospheric lighting (golden hour, dramatic shadows)
- Surfaces and textures (wood, fabric, architectural detail)

**Banned on Instagram:**
- Generic stock imagery
- Boring centered compositions
- Tonal palettes that blend into feed (always have contrast)
- Text-only images without visual hook

## X / Twitter

**Tone:** News-driven, immediate, brevity-first. Fast-scrolling feed.

| Category | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Color palette** | High-contrast bold colors. Black/white, red/white, blue/white. Solid blocks, not gradients. |
| **Composition** | 1200×627 (1.91:1 landscape) for link/news, or 1080×1080 (1:1) for standalone. |
| **Text overlay** | 1-2 words max. Bold sans-serif. Immediately readable. |
| **Focal point** | Centered. Bold. Strong contrast. Large headlines. |

**What catches the eye:**
- Single dramatic image with one clear focal point
- News-graphic aesthetic (clean, minimal, high contrast)
- Faces with strong expressions (but no real people)
- Bold geometric compositions

**Banned on X:**
- Subtle gradients
- Multiple subjects competing
- Tiny text
- Slow-reading compositions (X users decide in <1s)

## TikTok / Reels (thumbnail + first frame)

**Tone:** Curiosity-driven, urgent, emotional. Action + surprise.

| Category | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Color palette** | Vibrant, saturated. Red = urgency. Yellow = excitement. High contrast. |
| **Composition** | 1080×1350 (4:5 portrait) or 1080×1080 (1:1). Portrait = more screen space. |
| **Text overlay** | 1-2 word hooks in bold stylized fonts. Under 30 words. ("Wait, What?" "Try This.") |
| **Focal point** | Dynamic, action-oriented subject. Pointing, surprised expression, motion. |

**What catches the eye:**
- Action poses (hand pointing, mid-motion)
- Surprise / shocked expressions
- Bold text overlays within 0.3s
- High contrast with bold colors
- Trending sounds + visual styles

**Banned on TikTok:**
- Static, slow-looking thumbnails
- Boring centered compositions
- No text on screen in first 0.5s
- Over-polished corporate look (kills native feel)

## YouTube Shorts

**Tone:** Authoritative, intriguing, visual hook in first 3s. Thumbnails matter most.

| Category | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Color palette** | High contrast. Bold. Strong colors. Often yellow/red for attention. |
| **Composition** | 1080×1920 (9:16 vertical). First frame = thumbnail-quality. |
| **Text overlay** | 3-5 words, bold, center-top or center-bottom. |
| **Focal point** | Direct eye contact or dramatic action. Pattern interrupt in first 0.5s. |

**What catches the eye:**
- Direct eye contact (if person shown)
- Strong contrast (light vs dark)
- Bold text overlay (3-5 words, readable at thumbnail size)
- Surprising first frame (unexpected subject, angle, or motion)
- Clear subject isolation

**Banned on YT Shorts:**
- Slow intros (kills retention in first 3s)
- Boring thumbnail-quality first frames
- Multiple focal points
- No text overlay (loses to videos with bold text)

## Threads (Meta)

**Tone:** Casual, lived-in, iPhone-aesthetic. Less polished = more reach.

| Category | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Color palette** | Muted, natural. iPhone-photo aesthetic. Less saturation than IG. |
| **Composition** | 1080×1080 (1:1) or 4:5. Casual framing. |
| **Text overlay** | Minimal. If any, 1-3 words max. |
| **Focal point** | Document-style. Single subject. Honest composition. |

**What catches the eye:**
- Real-feeling imagery (slightly imperfect, natural light)
- Document/observational aesthetic
- Architectural / environmental shots
- Quiet compositions

**Banned on Threads:**
- Over-polished studio look
- Heavily branded compositions
- Aggressive CTAs in the image itself

## Cross-platform image checklist

Before approving any generated image:

- [ ] High contrast between subject and background
- [ ] One clear focal point (not competing elements)
- [ ] Under 30 words of text overlay (or none)
- [ ] Text within center "safe zone" (not edges)
- [ ] No logos, no real people's faces
- [ ] Mobile-readable (test at thumbnail size on smallest screen)
- [ ] Aspect ratio correct for platform
- [ ] Matches the platform's tonal expectations (see per-platform rules)
- [ ] Visual matches the caption's hook (one reinforces the other)
