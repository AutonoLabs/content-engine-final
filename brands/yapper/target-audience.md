# Target Audience — Yapper

---

## Primary audience (who we want most)

**Role / identity:** Senior care facility operators, administrators, and activity directors at ALFs, SNFs, memory care units, and home health agencies. Multi-facility operators looking for portfolio-wide visibility.

**Age range:** 35-65 (facility administrators, activity directors, clinical leads)

**Geography:** US-focused (pilot partners: Marquis, AristaCare, Siskiyou Springs, Yorkshire Village)

**Pain points:** Staff can't check in with every resident every day. Residents have things to say that no one has bandwidth to hear, log, or act on. Satisfaction surveys don't get filled out. Red flags get missed. Families want updates but staff don't have time. Phones aren't in every room.

**What they care about:** Resident experience, compliance, operational efficiency, family communication, staff workload, differentiation from other facilities.

**What they're skeptical of:** AI hype, "tech for seniors" that's patronizing, products that create more work, anything that sounds like it replaces human care.

**Where they hang out:** LinkedIn (research, B2B), Instagram (facility life, resident stories), Facebook (community groups, facility marketing), TikTok (behind-the-scenes, activity content), industry conferences, trade publications (McKnight's, Senior Housing News)

---

## Secondary audience (who we want next)

**Role / identity:** Adult children with parents in senior care. Looking for peace of mind and connection.

**Age range:** 30-55

**Pain points:** Can't visit every day. Don't know how Mom/Dad is really doing. Guilt. Trust issues with facility.

**Where they hang out:** Instagram, Facebook, TikTok (family/caregiver content), Google (research)

**Note:** Yapper for Families is "Coming Soon" — content for this audience should build awareness, not drive conversion yet.

## Tertiary (who we don't target but might find us)

- Senior care industry journalists and analysts
- Health tech investors

## Platform-by-platform target mapping

### LinkedIn
**Primary on this platform:** Facility operators, administrators, multi-facility executives, activity directors
**Tone:** Founder-to-operator. Operational clarity. Dignified. Specific product language. Not corporate SaaS.
**Complexity:** Industry shorthand OK (ALF, SNF, memory care, PCC, red flag protocols). Regulatory awareness (HIPAA, compliance).
**What to ignore:** Generic B2B marketing content. Consumer wellness influencers. Platform-baseline B2B users who aren't in senior care.

### X / Twitter
**Primary on this platform:** Senior care thought leaders, health tech community, Yapper's founding team personal networks
**Tone:** Punchy, observational, specific. Founder voice. Not thread-bro.
**Complexity:** Industry context with brief explanation for outsiders.
**What to ignore:** Crypto/web3. Generic AI hype. Productivity Twitter.

### Instagram
**Primary on this platform:** Activity directors, facility marketing teams, adult children researching care
**Tone:** Visual storytelling. Resident moments (with permission). Warm. Human. Behind-the-scenes.
**Complexity:** Light on jargon. Focus on the moment — the call, the smile, the story.
**What to ignore:** Pure B2B decision-makers (they're on LinkedIn). Clinical/medical content.

### TikTok
**Primary on this platform:** Activity directors sharing content, younger family caregivers, facility staff
**Tone:** Casual native. Behind-the-scenes. Quick moments. Real.
**Complexity:** Show-don't-tell. Low jargon.
**What to ignore:** Pure entertainment. Dance trends (unless authentically tied to facility life).

### YouTube (Shorts + long-form)
**Primary on this platform:** Facility operators researching solutions, activity directors learning best practices
**Tone:** Authoritative + accessible. Demo-forward. Show the product.
**Complexity:** Mid-jargon OK with brief context.
**What to ignore:** Pure entertainment. Gaming.

### Threads
**Primary on this platform:** Senior care community overflow, casual industry chat
**Tone:** Most casual register. Like talking to a smart friend in care.
**Complexity:** Industry shorthand OK.
**What to ignore:** Generic productivity Twitter. AI hype.

### Facebook
**Primary on this platform:** Facility community groups, older family members, local facility marketing
**Tone:** Accessible, community-oriented, slightly more traditional register
**Complexity:** Plain English. No jargon.
**What to ignore:** B2B decision-makers (they're on LinkedIn).

## Tone calibration rules

| Gap | Voice adjustment |
|---|---|
| Brand target is narrower than platform baseline | Get specific. Use senior care shorthand. Talk like an insider. |
| Brand target is older than platform baseline (Facebook) | Slow down. More context. Plain English. |
| Brand target is younger than platform baseline (TikTok) | Faster cuts. More native. Visual-first. Show the moment. |
| Brand target is more B2B than platform baseline (LinkedIn) | Operational register. ROI logic. Compliance awareness. |
| Brand target is more consumer than platform baseline (Instagram, Facebook) | Lifestyle language. Emotional hooks. Visual storytelling. |
| Brand target is more female than platform baseline | Listen-first. Visual quality high. Avoid bro-isms. |
