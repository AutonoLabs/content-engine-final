# Yapper Attention & Retention Playbook

**Purpose:** Make every Yapper post stop the scroll, earn attention, remain credible, and create one clear next action—without fear, clinical framing, or AI hype.

## 1. The Yapper content sequence

Every post follows:

1. **Stop:** A visual or opening line understood in under one second.
2. **Stay:** A tension, contrast, or unanswered question that earns the next line.
3. **Understand:** One concrete idea, mechanism, or story.
4. **Remember:** A distinctive Yapper phrase, image, or emotional beat.
5. **Act:** One soft, specific next step.

Do not attempt to communicate multiple pillars in one post.

## 2. Caption architecture

### Opening window

The first two visible lines must work before “See more.” They should contain a complete, audience-specific tension—not a greeting, setup, or generic inspiration.

Strong opening types:

- **Contrarian:** “Senior living doesn’t need another app.”
- **Contrast:** “The resident hears a phone call. The operator sees a pattern.”
- **Specific observation:** “The residents who need to be heard most are not always the ones who complain.”
- **Moment:** “At 9:03 a.m., the phone rang. Tom picked up.”
- **Question:** “What can one ordinary phone call tell you about an entire community?”
- **Hidden mechanism:** “The technology disappears the moment the resident answers.”

Avoid:

- “We’re excited to announce…”
- “In today’s fast-paced world…”
- generic quotations
- unsupported statistics
- “You won’t believe…”
- fear, guilt, or catastrophe framing
- excessive cliffhangers that withhold the actual value

### Body

Use one idea per paragraph and short paragraphs for mobile scanning.

Recommended working ranges—not universal algorithm rules:

- **Image post:** roughly 300–700 characters
- **Short video:** roughly 150–450 characters
- **Founder/operator insight:** roughly 600–1,200 characters when the idea genuinely earns the space

The post should be as long as the insight requires and no longer. Exact optimal length is audience- and format-dependent; test ranges rather than assuming one magic number.

### Retention pattern

1. Hook
2. Re-hook or contrast
3. Concrete mechanism/story
4. Payoff
5. One CTA

Useful retention tools:

- open a loop, then close it within the post
- alternate short and medium sentences
- use a three-beat rhythm sparingly
- include one specific physical detail
- reveal the product after the human situation is clear
- use bullets only when they improve comprehension

### CTA

Use one CTA only:

- “See how it works: https://yapper.care/”
- “What does your community hear between scheduled activities?”
- “Which resident-engagement signal is hardest for your team to see?”

Do not combine visit, comment, share, book, follow, and download asks.

## 3. Static-image rules

A Yapper image must communicate its subject in under one second.

### Required

- one emotionally legible person or one unmistakable product action
- phone/receiver interaction large enough to read on mobile
- one dominant focal point
- clean negative space for a short headline or wordmark
- authentic expression, skin, hands, phone geometry, and cord
- mobile-first 4:5 composition for feed posts
- real environmental detail: books, mug, chair, window light, corridor, workstation

### Color hierarchy

- **Ink Plum #241C36:** authority and dark surfaces
- **Lavender #7B61AD / Lavender 700 #4E3E71:** dominant brand recognition
- **Lavender Mist #F4F0FA:** breathing room and warmth
- **Mint #98FFD9 / Mint Deep #3ECF9B:** small accent, state change, or focal cue

Mint is an accent, not a large text-bearing background. Use contrast intentionally: ink plum against lavender mist; white against ink plum; mint only to direct the eye.

### Text overlays

- Public Sans
- ideally 3–8 words
- one message, not a miniature caption
- large enough to read without opening the post
- high contrast
- no paragraph text inside images

### Visual hook families

- close-up receiver being lifted
- phone ringing in an otherwise still room
- authentic face changing from anticipation to recognition
- familiar rotary phone beside a modern operator dashboard
- empty chair + ringing phone as a curiosity beat
- direct-to-camera operator/founder with one clear on-screen point

Avoid sadness stock imagery, hospital cues, helplessness, excessive logo size, generic smiling seniors, neon AI glows, floating interfaces, purple-to-blue gradients, and cluttered dashboards.

## 4. Video rules

### Awareness videos

Default to 15–30 seconds. Deeper human stories may run 31–60 seconds when the narrative earns it.

### First three seconds

Show the idea before explaining it:

- **0.0–1.0s:** pattern interrupt—phone ring, hand entering frame, receiver lifting, abrupt close-up, direct gaze
- **1.0–3.0s:** concise hook text or spoken line
- no logo animation before the hook
- no slow establishing shot unless the silence itself is the pattern interrupt

### Retention structure

- **0–3s:** hook
- **3–8s:** human context
- **8–15s:** mechanism or reveal
- **15–23s:** emotional/business payoff
- **final 2–3s:** one CTA/end card

### Production

- vertical or mobile-native framing where appropriate
- readable captions for sound-off viewing
- concise on-screen supers
- dynamic camera movement only when it adds meaning
- ambient sound, a real phone ring, and a restrained warm chime
- no generic corporate music beds
- authentic expressions over staged product demonstrations

## 6. Production scorecard

Score each category 0–2. Do not schedule below 8/10.

| Category | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hook | Generic | Relevant but safe | Specific tension/contrast immediately |
| Visual stop | Cluttered/generic | Attractive | One clear, emotionally legible focal point |
| Retention | Flat explanation | Some structure | Open loop → reveal → payoff |
| Credibility | Hype/unsupported | Plausible | Concrete, approved, dignity-first |
| CTA | Missing/multiple | Generic | One clear, soft next step |

Additional hard gates:

- claims verified
- no resident/facility-private information
- no medical-device implication
- Yapper palette and wordmark correct
- hands, phone, text, and UI visually checked
- correct company-page target and schedule verified

## 7. Testing plan

Change one major variable at a time:

1. Hook type: contrast vs observation vs story
2. Creative: human face vs phone/product action vs operator insight
3. Format: static vs short video vs carousel
4. CTA: website vs question

Track:

- impressions/reach
- click-throughs
- comments with substance, not only reactions
- video completion/view-through where available
- follower growth
- qualified website visits
- removals or negative quality signals

After 12 posts, promote the best-performing hook + creative combinations into repeatable templates. Do not infer a rule from one post.
