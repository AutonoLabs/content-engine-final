# Yapper Cross-Platform Attention Playbook

**Goal:** Win the first frame, retain attention, create meaningful interaction, and remain recognizable without making every Yapper post look or sound the same.

The “0.1 second” idea is a useful design mindset, not a universal measured platform rule. Our operating standard: **the first frame and first line must be understandable instantly on a phone.**

## Core principle

**Repeat structures, not surfaces.**

Keep consistent:

- Yapper’s warm, dignified, non-clinical voice
- Public Sans
- Lavender, Lavender 700, Ink Plum, Mint, Mint Deep, Lavender Mist
- ordinary-phone interaction
- authentic human emotion
- verified claims only
- one clear CTA

Rotate:

- narrator and audience
- hook type
- visual focal point
- camera language
- format
- pacing
- audio treatment
- interaction mechanic

## Universal first-frame test

Before publishing, view the asset at small mobile size and ask:

1. Can I identify the subject in under one second?
2. Is there one dominant face, object, movement, or line?
3. Does the first frame create tension, recognition, or curiosity?
4. Does it work muted?
5. Is the brand recognizable without a large logo?
6. Is the next action obvious but not pushy?

Failing any of the first four means revise before scheduling.

# 1. Instagram

## What Instagram says matters

Instagram says Reels are initially shown to a small audience of followers and non-followers, then expanded when early viewers respond. Signals include watch time, retention, shares, likes, and comments. Instagram recommends:

- a compelling hook in the first three seconds
- stand-alone content that makes sense to non-followers
- relatable, shareable ideas
- audio where appropriate
- regular experimentation
- monitoring three-second View Rate

## Yapper Reel formula

**Format:** 9:16, generally 15–30 seconds.

- 0.0–1.0s: phone rings, receiver lifts, direct gaze, or visual contradiction
- 1.0–3.0s: 3–8-word hook
- 3–10s: one human situation
- 10–20s: reveal Yapper’s mechanism
- final 2–3s: quiet end card + one CTA

### Reel hooks

- “No app required.”
- “She already knows how to use it.”
- “What the resident hears…”
- “What the operator sees…”
- “One ordinary phone. Two very different experiences.”

## Instagram carousels

- Slide 1: one promise, contrast, or question
- Slide 2: deepen the tension
- Slides 3–6: one idea per slide
- Final slide: payoff + save/share-worthy summary
- Keep text large and minimal; use imagery, diagrams, and short statements

Best Yapper carousel uses:

- “Resident view / operator view” split narrative
- “Five questions to ask before adding resident technology”
- “What happens during a Yapper call”
- “One phone call, four signals” only when claims are verified
- memory-prompt sequences

## Instagram interaction

Optimize for shares, saves, thoughtful comments, and profile visits—not likes alone.

Use CTAs such as:

- “Save this for your next resident-engagement planning session.”
- “Which part of the resident experience creates the most friction?”
- “Send this to someone designing technology for older adults.”

Stories can use polls, question stickers, and two-choice operator questions. Avoid manipulative engagement bait.

## Working caption range

- Reels: 1–5 short lines; the video must stand alone
- Carousels: enough context to deepen the slides, generally 150–500 characters to start testing

# 2. TikTok

## What TikTok says matters

TikTok’s official guidance recommends:

- TikTok-first, vertical, high-resolution creative
- a clear proposition in the first three seconds
- the strongest hook within the first six seconds
- Hook → Body → Close structure
- text overlays/captions for context
- roughly 5–10 words per second when showing text
- movement, transitions, graphics, and sound
- materially different creative variants rather than tiny changes
- refreshing creative as fatigue appears

## Yapper TikTok formula

Yapper should feel native and personal—not like a polished LinkedIn ad cropped vertically.

- Open mid-action or mid-sentence
- Use direct-to-camera family/operator POV
- Show the phone early
- Use a real ring, receiver click, room tone, or original voiceover
- Keep cuts purposeful and human
- Close with one question or one soft CTA

### Native Yapper formats

1. Adult-child confession: “I kept buying Mom things with screens…”
2. Operator observation: “The residents who need support don’t always ask for it.”
3. First-call moment: phone rings → answer → expression changes
4. Comment-reply video
5. Myth/reality: “Older adults won’t use AI” versus “They don’t need to use an interface.”
6. Build-in-public clip from the Yapper team
7. Phone-ring ASMR hook
8. “What residents hear / what operators see” split screen

## Interaction

- End with a specific, answerable question
- Reply quickly and conversationally
- Turn strong comments into response videos
- Avoid pretending employees are unaffiliated users

## Working duration and caption

- Start testing 15–30-second clips
- Caption: usually one concise context line plus one question; the video carries the story

TikTok is not currently connected in Blotato, so this remains a prepared channel until account access is added.

# 3. YouTube Shorts

## What YouTube says matters

YouTube’s official Shorts guidance recommends:

- vertical 9:16
- capturing attention in the first few seconds
- getting the message across quickly
- clear titles
- relevant hashtags, not tag stuffing
- comments and creator interaction
- using analytics to determine what resonates
- linking viewers to related content

Shorts may run up to three minutes, but maximum length is not a target.

## Yapper Shorts formula

YouTube should deliver a complete miniature story or explanation with a clear payoff.

- First frame: movement or a visually clear question
- 0–2s: title promise
- 2–8s: setup
- 8–20s: demonstration or tension
- 20–30s: payoff
- final beat: related video/site CTA

### Shorts series

- “How Yapper works in 30 seconds”
- “A resident’s first call”
- “One dashboard signal explained”
- “Technology for seniors: one design rule”
- “Questions operators should ask before adopting AI”
- “What the phone call does—and does not do”

## Interaction

- Use a title that states the benefit or question clearly
- Pin a question or clarification
- Respond to useful comments
- Connect Shorts into recurring series rather than isolated uploads

## Working duration

- Start with 20–40 seconds
- Use 45–60 seconds only when the story earns it

# 4. X

## What X says matters

X’s business guidance emphasizes:

- clear, concise copy
- limited text in images
- movement in the first seconds of video
- videos around 15 seconds or less for high completion/branding use cases
- a clear CTA when driving action

## Yapper X formula

X rewards a sharp thought more than a polished mini-essay.

- One contrarian sentence + one proof/mechanism line
- One image or very short video
- Minimal hashtags: generally zero to two
- Use threads only when each post advances the argument

### X formats

1. Contrarian operator take
2. Before/after statement
3. One approved stat + visual
4. Product design principle
5. Founder build note
6. Screenshot/feature reveal with sanitized UI
7. Two-post contrast: resident experience / operator intelligence
8. Short phone-ring loop

Example:

> Senior living doesn’t need another app.
>
> Yapper puts the intelligence behind a phone call residents already understand.

## Interaction

- Ask one precise question
- Reply with useful specifics
- Quote-post industry news with a distinct perspective
- Avoid generic hashtag clusters and duplicated LinkedIn prose

## Working length

- Prefer one complete thought in roughly 70–220 characters
- Use 3–5-post threads for deeper explanation

# 5. Threads

## What Meta says matters

Meta says Threads is designed for fresh perspectives and conversation. Its internal data indicates posts with tagged topics generally receive more views than posts without one. Threads offers detailed views, replies, quotes, reposts, follower-growth, and link-click insights.

## Yapper Threads formula

Threads should sound like an informed person starting a conversation—not a company broadcasting a campaign.

- Open with an observation or tension
- Use one relevant Topic Tag
- Keep the post conversational
- End with an answerable question
- Stay active in replies

### Threads formats

1. “Something we keep noticing…” observation
2. Operator question
3. Product-design debate
4. Short founder/build note
5. One-sentence myth correction
6. Memory prompt inviting stories
7. Quote-response to an industry conversation
8. Two- or three-post mini-thread

Example:

> Something we keep getting wrong in age-tech: we ask the resident to adapt to the interface.
>
> What if the interface were simply a phone call?
>
> What technology has actually worked in your community—and why?
>
> Topic: Senior Living

## Interaction

Replies are part of the content. A Threads post without follow-up participation is incomplete.

## Working length

- Start with 80–300 characters
- Use longer posts only for distinctive stories or expert viewpoints

# 6. Reddit

## What Reddit says matters

Reddit’s official organic playbook says businesses should:

- listen first
- understand each community’s rules
- comment before posting
- add real value
- communicate like an individual, not corporate copy
- disclose identity/affiliation
- comment frequently and post sparingly
- avoid self-promotion
- speak to moderators before posting branded content

## Yapper Reddit formula

Reddit is not a distribution channel for our social graphics. It is a listening and expertise channel.

- Start with helpful comments
- Mention affiliation when relevant
- Do not force Yapper into a conversation
- Answer the question before mentioning the product
- No fake family/caregiver personas
- No resident-private information
- Use ReddGrow only for discovery and drafting; human review remains mandatory

### Reddit content

- detailed answers to technology/aging questions
- operator design lessons
- consent/privacy considerations
- telephone accessibility insights
- transparent founder/operator AMA only after community credibility and moderator approval

## Interaction

Measure upvote rate, substantive replies, brand mentions, referral traffic, and moderator/account-health signals.

# Variety system

## 12-format rotation

1. Cinematic human micro-story
2. Adult-child UGC/confession
3. Operator/expert direct-to-camera
4. Feature tutorial
5. Myth versus reality
6. Approved-stat visual
7. Resident/operator split screen
8. Phone-ring/audio-led hook
9. Founder build-in-public note
10. Checklist carousel
11. Comment-reply video
12. Sanitized product walkthrough

## Hook rotation

- contrast
- question
- specific moment
- observation
- reveal
- test/demonstration
- sound
- direct gaze
- surprising object/action
- credible statistic

Do not repeat the same hook family or visual format twice in succession.

## Creative portfolio

- **60% proven structures:** repeat the best-performing format with new stories/angles
- **30% controlled experiments:** new narrator, hook, camera language, or interaction mechanic
- **10% wildcards:** culturally timely, unusual, or highly creative concepts

This preserves consistency without creative sameness.

# Cross-platform adaptation example

**Core idea:** Residents should not have to learn another interface.

- **LinkedIn:** operator insight + human portrait
- **Instagram Reel:** receiver lifts; “No app required”
- **Instagram carousel:** resident view versus operator view
- **TikTok:** adult daughter confession or direct-to-camera operator POV
- **YouTube Short:** 30-second “how it works” explainer
- **X:** two-line contrarian statement + image
- **Threads:** conversational question about age-tech design
- **Reddit:** detailed, disclosed answer to a real accessibility question

Never copy-paste one caption everywhere.

# Measurement by platform

## Instagram

- three-second View Rate
- average watch time/completion
- shares and saves
- profile visits
- comments with substance

## TikTok

- early hold
- average watch time
- completion and rewatches
- shares
- comments and response-video opportunities

## YouTube Shorts

- viewed versus swiped away
- audience-retention curve
- average percentage viewed
- subscribers and comments

## X

- impressions
- media views/completion
- link/profile clicks
- replies and reposts

## Threads

- views
- replies, quotes, reposts
- link visits
- follower growth
- discovery source

## Reddit

- upvote rate
- useful replies
- brand mentions
- referral traffic
- moderation/account-health signals

After 12–20 posts per platform, promote winning hook + format combinations into templates. Do not infer a platform rule from one post.
