# Platform Hooks — Stop the Scroll

> Per-platform scroll-stopping techniques for the first 0.5-3 seconds. Engineered into every caption generation. Updated 2026-07-07.

---

## Why this doc exists

Good writing is necessary but insufficient. The viewer's thumb moves at ~0.5s. If the hook doesn't land, the content is wasted — even if it's brilliant below the fold.

This doc is the canonical reference for **hook engineering**. Every platform prompt references this. Every caption draft must demonstrate a hook from this doc's playbook in its first line.

---

## LinkedIn (first 0.5-2s)

**First line target:** 7-10 words, ideally under 12. Mobile shows ~140 characters (~2-3 lines) before the fold.

**Format rules:**
- ✅ Unicode bold first line
- ✅ Italic for key phrase inside the hook
- ✅ Short paragraphs, generous white space
- ✅ Strategic emoji for mobile readability (+40%)
- ❌ Corporate openers ("I'm excited to announce...", "I want to share something...")

**5 hook patterns that work:**

| Pattern | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Contrarian / pattern interrupt | "Not everyone deserves a personal brand." | Challenges belief, creates tension |
| Specific / data-driven | "I made €7,800 from one post." | Numbers grab attention instantly |
| Emotional / personal reveal | "I almost quit everything last week." | Vulnerability, shared struggle |
| Question-based | "Why do 90% of LinkedIn posts go unread?" | Forces cognitive engagement |
| Bold statement | "I doubled my engagement with one small tweak." | Disrupts scrolling rhythm |

**Algorithm demotes:**
- Clickbait without payoff
- Hooks buried below 3+ paragraphs
- Vague or generic openers

**Mobile-fold check:** After drafting the hook, paste it into a notes app on your phone at the smallest font setting. Does it land in 1 second?

## TikTok / Instagram Reels (first 0.5-1s)

**Video structure rules:**
- First frame = pattern interrupt (high contrast, sudden motion, unexpected facial expression)
- Text overlay within 0.3s (strong short question or bold claim)
- Audio hook (sudden sound, voiceover question, beat drop)
- Combine all 3 for maximum effect

**Optimal length:** 6-15 seconds. Under 6s for hooks. Retention drops 100% → 10-15% in first second if hook fails.

**Swipe triggers (avoid):**
- Generic visuals (stock-feel)
- Slow intros / fades
- No text on screen in first 0.5s
- Too much "talking head" without motion

**Text-on-screen formulas (3-5 words max):**
- "Wait — what?" (curiosity)
- "This is illegal." (controversy)
- "Watch what happens." (suspense)
- "Why does this work?" (question)
- "[Number] things about [topic]" (list promise)

## X / Twitter (first 1-2s)

**Algorithm weights:** Replies > retweets > likes. The viral trigger is 10+ engagements in first 15 minutes.

**7 hook formulas that drive replies + impressions:**

| Formula | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Number promise | "I have written 500+ threads. Here are the 7 hook formulas that actually get reads." | Specific numbers = credibility |
| Contrarian take | "Everyone says post daily to grow on X. That advice nearly killed my account." | Challenges belief, creates tension |
| Transformation | "6 months ago I had 200 followers. Today I have 40,000. I did exactly 5 things." | Before/after aspiration |
| Curiosity gap | "There is one tweet structure that 10x'd my reach. Almost nobody uses it." | Open loop forces engagement |
| Mistake list | "I wasted 2 years making these 6 mistakes on X so you do not have to." | Specific number + vulnerability |
| How-to / repeatable system | "How to write a Twitter thread that gets 100k impressions (a repeatable system)." | Clear actionable path |
| Bold claim + proof | "Threads are not dead. This one did 2M views last week." | Trust via tangible proof |

**Structure rule:** One idea per tweet. Sub-hook at the top of every tweet in a thread.

## YouTube Shorts (first 1-3s)

**3-second hook framework:**

1. **0-0.5s:** Visual pattern interrupt (eye contact, sudden movement, high-contrast text overlay)
2. **0.5-1.5s:** Verbal hook OR text-on-screen question/claim
3. **1.5-3s:** Promise delivery begins

**Title formulas:**
- "Everything you've been told about [X] is wrong."
- "If you're a [target audience], listen to this."
- "Why is your [problem] getting worse?"
- "The 1 rule that fixes [problem] instantly."
- "Do this in 10 seconds to [result]."

**Text overlay:** 3-5 words, bold, center-top or center-bottom.

**Audio rules:**
- Fast pacing, cuts every 2-3 seconds
- Start with high energy
- Trending sounds boost algorithmic reach

**Thumbnail rules (for long-form, not shorts):**
- Direct eye contact
- High contrast
- 3-4 words max, large text
- Show result first ("After this 10-sec tip...")

## Instagram (static image posts, first 0.5s)

**Image requirements:**
- High contrast, clean composition
- One focal point (no clutter)
- Text on image if it's a quote/carousel — short, readable on mobile

**Caption first line:** Same rules as LinkedIn hook — bold, short, contrarian or specific. First line shows ~125 chars on mobile before "...more" cutoff.

---

## Threads (Meta, first 0.5-1s)

**Format:** Conversational, less polished than LinkedIn. Threads rewards dialogue.

**Hooks:**
- Casual questions ("anyone else...?", "do you guys...")
- Strong opinions stated plainly
- Short observations
- Threads (multi-post) work well for storytelling

**Avoid:** Corporate tone, hashtags (Threads algorithm doesn't weight them), links in main post (pushes reach to comments only).

---

## Cross-platform hook checklist

Before publishing any post, verify:

- [ ] First line is under 12 words (LinkedIn) / under 8 words (X) / under 30 chars (TikTok text)
- [ ] Hook uses one of the 5-7 patterns from this doc
- [ ] No corporate opener phrases
- [ ] No vague statements ("I want to share...")
- [ ] Mobile-readable (test on smallest screen)
- [ ] Contrarian / specific / emotional / question / bold — at least one
- [ ] First line creates unresolved curiosity (the reader needs to know what comes next)
