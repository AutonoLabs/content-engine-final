## Caption

Most UK construction contracts hold back 5% of the project value until snag-free handover.

On a £60k kitchen-and-extension job, that's £3,000 sitting in escrow for six months after the builder leaves. Six months during which the builder has zero incentive to come back for the snagging list. Six months during which the client holds a contractor's money without a mechanism to release it.

Retention without a release mechanism is a complaint that hasn't started yet.

AllSquared handles retention inside the same FCA-regulated escrow account that holds the rest of the milestone payments. Builder gets the 5% on snagging sign-off, not six months later when they've moved to the next job. Client gets the snagging list closed before the retention is released.

The mechanism is the protection.

—

What changes when retention has a deadline built into the platform, not just the contract?
