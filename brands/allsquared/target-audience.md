# Target Audience — AllSquared

> Per-brand override layer. Sits between platform demographics and voice profile.

---

## Target audience

**Primary audience (who we want most):**
- Role / identity: UK construction project owners commissioning £20k-£200k projects (kitchen refits, bathroom installs, extensions, full renovations). Also UK agencies and producers doing large milestone-based client work.
- Age range: 35-55, skewed slightly older than LinkedIn baseline
- Geography: UK, focus on London + SE + Midlands
- Income bracket: £60k-£200k household, willing to spend £20k-£200k on home/commercial projects
- Pain points: Got burned by contractor who took deposit + disappeared. Doesn't trust platforms with unregulated escrow. Has heard horror stories about dispute resolution taking 6-8 weeks and losing money to "fees."
- Where they hang out: LinkedIn (research, vetting), Checkatrade + TrustMark (vetting), Instagram (project inspiration), Facebook Groups (local recommendations), YouTube (how-tos), trade forums (industry-specific)

**Secondary audience (who we want next):**
- Role / identity: UK builders/trades doing £50k+ projects + small agency owners doing milestone-based client work
- Age range: 30-55, mostly self-employed or small agency owners (1-10 people)
- Geography: UK
- Pain points: Lost money on client disputes. Wants payment protection without expensive legal infrastructure. Wants clients to trust them enough to commit to £20k+ projects.
- Where they hang out: LinkedIn, trade forums (Checkatrade Pro, MyBuilder, trade Facebook groups), Instagram (showing work), TikTok (how-tos + project reveals), YouTube (longer trade tutorials)

**Tertiary (who we don't target but might find us):**
- UK property developers doing new-build fit-outs
- Estate agents facilitating high-value transactions
- Estate planning solicitors, financial advisors (referral sources)

## Platform-by-platform target mapping

### LinkedIn

**Primary on this platform:** UK construction project owners (decision-makers) + UK small agency owners doing £50k+ projects + UK property developers
**Tone:** Business register, regulatory clarity, founder-to-founder. NOT corporate-to-customer. Don't talk down to them — they know the trade.
**Complexity:** FCA references ok, construction-trade vocabulary ok (milestone, retention, snagging, JCT contracts), financial terms ok
**What to ignore:** Generic B2B content marketing tips. Platform baseline users who aren't in construction/trades. Don't try to be everything to everyone.

### X / Twitter

**Primary on this platform:** UK construction + property Twitter community + property journalists + trade trade-press writers
**Tone:** Punchy, contrarian, news-anchored. Don't be corporate. Don't be salesy. Be the person saying the thing nobody else will say in the industry.
**Complexity:** Industry shorthand ok (FCA, NHBC, JCT contracts, retention, defects period). Brief context for outsiders.
**What to ignore:** Crypto/web3 audience. Generic productivity content. Self-improvement Twitter. Pure consumer trend-chasing.

### Instagram

**Primary on this platform:** UK homeowners researching projects (visual inspiration + project reveal aesthetic)
**Tone:** Visual storytelling, project reveal aesthetic, lifestyle context. The visual does most of the work.
**Complexity:** Light on jargon, focus on the finished result + the journey. Regulatory details go in the caption if anywhere.
**What to ignore:** Pure B2B decision-makers (they're on LinkedIn). Pure aesthetic/decor audience. Trend-chasing content.

### TikTok

**Primary on this platform:** Trade apprentices + DIY-curious homeowners + young builders learning the trade
**Tone:** Casual native, fast cuts, behind-the-scenes of the trade. Show the work.
**Complexity:** Low jargon, show-don't-tell. Let the visuals explain.
**What to ignore:** Pure entertainment audiences, dance trends, AI hype content.

### YouTube (Shorts + long-form)

**Primary on this platform:** Trade professionals learning new techniques + homeowners researching how-to + project managers showing their process
**Tone:** Authoritative + accessible, longer explanations ok. Show the work + explain why.
**Complexity:** Mid-jargon ok with brief context (e.g., "snagging — the list of defects at handover").
**What to ignore:** Pure entertainment, gaming adjacent, general lifestyle.

### Threads

**Primary on this platform:** UK construction Twitter overflow + casual industry chat + trade community banter
**Tone:** Most casual register, like talking to a smart friend in the trade. Lowercase ok. Fragments ok.
**Complexity:** Trade shorthand ok. Don't over-explain.
**What to ignore:** Generic productivity Twitter, AI hype, consumer trend-chasing.

### Facebook

**Primary on this platform:** Older homeowners 45+ + local community groups (Nextdoor, neighborhood Facebook Groups) + trade-specific Facebook Groups
**Tone:** Accessible, community-oriented, slightly more traditional register. Plain English.
**Complexity:** Plain English, no jargon. Plain talk about how the platform works.
**What to ignore:** B2B decision-makers, pure consumer trend-chasers, brand-aspirational content.

## Tone calibration (AllSquared-specific)

| Gap | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Brand target is **narrower** than platform baseline | Get specific. Use industry shorthand (FCA, JCT, retention, snagging). Don't explain basics. Talk like an insider. |
| Brand target is **more B2B** than platform baseline | Business register. ROI logic. Regulatory clarity. |
| Brand target is **higher-income** than platform baseline | Premium register. Quality > quantity. No budget talk. |
| Brand target is **more consumer** on Instagram/TikTok | Lifestyle language. Visual storytelling. Emotional hooks about "peace of mind" not "compliance." |
